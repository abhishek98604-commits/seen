Seen.
An open exercise in looking — for artists, writers, and the quietly curious.
What is this? A photograph appears. You look — really look — for two or three minutes. Then you see how an AI observed the same scene. Compare. Notice what you caught that it missed, and what slipped past you.

One thing to know: this AI was calibrated as a normal, attentive observer — not a professional or critic. This is not a test. It is a mirror for how you naturally see.
How to look — about 2 minutes
1Look at the photograph. Don't analyse yet — just feel what it does to you first.
2Find the subject. What is it doing, or not doing? Where does it sit in the frame?
3Follow the light. Where does it land? What does it leave in shadow?
4Listen. If this image had sound, what would you hear?
5Feel the mood. Stillness, tension, warmth — what hangs in the air?
